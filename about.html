Let me tell you about myself.
Hello, my name is Gerardo Montemayor. I grew up in Clarksville, Maryland and am currently a sophomore at Cornell University studying computer science. I've always enjoyed solving problems and I enjoy applying those skills to software programming.
My Journey Into CS
I first started learning about computer science in early quarantine. I took an introductory course in Cybersecurity at the Johns Hopkins STEM Academy. In highschool, I took a few other CS courses and learned Java.
Coming into college, I still wasn't sure what I wanted to study, but after taking my first class in Python, I was immediately drawn to the study. I realized that I enjoyed writing programs and seeing them work right in front of me. So I decided to continue in this direction, learning python and diving more into Java my freshman year.
Now, I'm excited to be taking even more advanced courses in computer science, having recently taken a functional programming course and soon to be taking Machine Learning and Algorithms. This past semester I worked on a several-month long project with a team where we programmed a Cornell-inspired Tower Defense Game. Check it out in my projects section!
